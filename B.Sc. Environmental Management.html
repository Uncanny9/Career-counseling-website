B.Sc. Environmental Management

Bachelor in Science (B. Sc.) in Environmental Management is introduced for the production of qualified environmental management professionals with leadership quality and problem-solving aptitude. The unique feature of this academic program is the integration of scientific, social, cultural, economic aspects of the environment. The Environmental Management degree builds on the understanding of the planning systems and processes of environmental changes and offers training in the management techniques required to put into effect the principle of sustainable development.

Objectives

The main objectives of the B.Sc. Programme are to:

  • Provide students with a comprehensive understanding of the environment, including its issues, problems, and its interconnectedness with other related fields.
  • Produce qualified environmental managers who are capable of making independent decisions on various environmental issues.
  • Familiarize and equip students with environmental management tools, enabling them to pursue higher studies and engage in research in the field.
  • Emphasize the application of environmental management concepts and environmental analysis in professional areas, ensuring that graduates are well-prepared to address environmental challenges in their chosen careers.

ELIGIBILITY

Candidates applying for the B.Sc. Programme must have successfully completed the Class 12 examination of NEB or an equivalent level in the Science and Technology stream. This stream includes disciplines such as Forestry, Agriculture, Veterinary/Engineering, among others, from a recognized academic institution. To be eligible for admission, candidates should have obtained a minimum score of 45% or a CGPA/GPA of 2.0 on a scale of 4.0, along with at least Grade C (or Grade D in the case of A-level) in the following subjects: Physics, Chemistry, or Environment-related subjects like Environmental Management or Pollution. Additionally, candidates should have achieved a minimum of 45% in either Mathematics, Biology, Zoology, Botany, or Computer Science. These eligibility requirements ensure that candidates have a solid foundation in relevant scientific subjects before enrolling in the B.Sc. Programme.

JOB PROSPECTS

Upon completion of the B.Sc. Degree, graduates can expect employment opportunities in various areas. They can work in national and local level organizations, ministries, departments, district offices, and municipalities, assuming roles as mid-level officers or managers. Consulting firms and research institutions also offer positions as research assistants. Private companies and public corporations are potential employers, providing opportunities for graduates to contribute in different capacities. National and international organizations, as well as community-based organizations (CBOs) focused on environmental issues, are additional avenues for employment. Furthermore, graduates can explore opportunities in environmental quality monitoring and assessment laboratories, utilizing their skills and knowledge in this specialized field. The diverse range of potential employment areas ensures that B.Sc. graduates have multiple career options to apply their expertise and make a meaningful contribution in the environmental sector.

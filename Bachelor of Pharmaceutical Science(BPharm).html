Bachelor of Pharmaceutical Science (BPharm)

Bachelor of Pharmacy is a four-year course introduced at Maharajgunj Campus under the Institute of Medicine (IOM) in 2000 AD. B.Pharm is a four-year course under Tribhuvan University (TU). Nine pharmacy colleges offer the Bachelor of Pharmacy (BPharm) course with affiliation from Tribhuvan University.

The Bachelor of Pharmacy course is a four-year program that focuses on professional pharmacy courses and supplementary subjects necessary for the field. The program aims to train and produce competent pharmacists capable of delivering pharmaceutical services in various capacities. Graduates of the program are prepared to work as community and hospital pharmacists, specializing in technology and research, managing pharmaceutical services, and specializing in different scientific aspects of pharmacy. The objective is to equip students with the knowledge and skills needed to excel in the pharmacy profession and contribute to the healthcare system.

The basic professional education will enable the graduates to demonstrate a broad understanding of the scientific principles and techniques of the pharmaceutical sciences. At the same time, it will also enable them to continue their studies to undertake postgraduate training and research. The program has been inspired by the values that pharmacists should be vital members of the healthcare team and they will hold a job either in the government or private sector in important areas such as community pharmacy, clinical pharmacy, hospital pharmacy, regulatory control, and drug management, pharmaceutical industry, quality assurance, academic activities, training activities, and research.

The primary goal of the program is to educate and train individuals to become competent and qualified pharmacists capable of providing pharmaceutical services in multiple roles. This includes preparing graduates to work as community and hospital pharmacists, serving the healthcare needs of the community and patients. The program also aims to develop specialists in technology and research, equipping them with the knowledge and skills to contribute to advancements in the field of pharmacy. Additionally, the program emphasizes the development of managerial abilities to prepare graduates for leadership roles in pharmaceutical services. Lastly, the program strives to cultivate specialists in various scientific aspects of pharmacy, fostering expertise in areas such as pharmaceutical sciences, drug development, and pharmacotherapy. Overall, the goal is to produce well-rounded pharmacists who can contribute effectively to the healthcare industry and improve patient outcomes.

Upon completion of the program, graduates will possess the necessary skills and knowledge to deliver quality services in the aforementioned areas. They will demonstrate professional competence as valuable members of the multiprofessional healthcare team, contributing to the overall patient care. With a broad understanding of the scientific foundation of pharmacy, they will be equipped to apply their knowledge effectively in practice, meeting the specific requirements of their respective fields. Additionally, graduates will excel as team members in collaborative research projects, leveraging their expertise to contribute to advancements in the field of pharmacy. They will showcase competence in information retrieval, handling, and integration, utilizing their knowledge, skills, and attitudes to solve professional problems efficiently. Overall, the program aims to produce capable professionals who can effectively contribute to the healthcare industry and provide optimal patient care.

ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for the program, students must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology subjects from a recognized educational institution and passed with a minimum of 50% or a CGPA of 2.4 in the Proficiency Certificate Level or Grade 12 Science Group.

Alternatively, students from A-level or other similar programs with different grading systems should have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology subjects and passed with a minimum of 50 percent or a CGPA of 2.4.

Bachelor of Computer Application

Bachelor of Computer Application (BCA) is a popular academic program among the students of the 21st century. This is an applied IT program with maximum IT credit hours offered at this level.

This BCA program of Pokhara University integrates IT with business and management, which adequately develops managerial skills with a modern business or organizational aspect. Upon completion of the program, graduates will be eligible for admission to MCA, MBA, MIS, MIT, MSc, and other master-level courses at recognized universities anywhere around the world.

Furthermore, BCA program graduates will have ample opportunities to work as IT Officers, system developers, system analysts, software engineers, system administrators, etc., within and outside the country.

The objectives of the BCA program are to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of both theoretical and practical aspects of computing. By the end of the program, students will have developed professional competence in software development, as well as the design and implementation of complex computer and information systems. They will possess a deep understanding of fundamental concepts in various areas of computer applications. Additionally, they will have the ability to analyze and synthesize information related to computer systems and applications and apply practical skills to effectively address problems that may arise in these areas. Overall, the program aims to equip students with the necessary knowledge and skills to excel in the field of computer applications.

ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for the program, candidates should have successfully completed the Class 12 examination from NEB or an equivalent level from a recognized academic institution. Additionally, they should have studied a course in Mathematics (Mathematics/Business Mathematics/Applied Mathematics) or Computer Science/Computer Application, which should have a minimum of 100 full marks or 5 credits in the SLCE (Class 11 or Class 12) examination of NEB or equivalent level. The candidates are required to have achieved a minimum score of 45% or a CGPA/GPA of 2.0 on a scale of 4.0, along with at least a Grade C (or Grade D for A-level) or a minimum of 45% in Mathematics/Computer Science. However, it is important to note that the minimum criterion of Grade C (or Grade D for A-level) or 45% does not apply to other courses.

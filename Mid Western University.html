Mid Western University

Mid-West University (MU) Nepal is an autonomous and public institution of higher learning with a mission to serve the people of Nepal and contribute to the global learning community by providing the benefits of higher education.

Established by the Government of Nepal through a Parliament Act on June 17, 2010 A.D., Mid-West University is supported by the Ministry of Education Nepal. It operates under the concept of a multi-university system and has constituent campuses and a Central Administration Office located in Birendranagar Municipality, Surkhet district, Nepal.

Mid-West University (MU) has received strong support from existing campuses in the region. The land, property, human resources, and students of the existing Tribhuvan University's affiliated campuses in the region have been merged under Mid-Western University.

The university offers a variety of academic programs at both undergraduate and graduate levels in fields such as Humanities and Social Sciences, Educational Sciences, Science and Technology, and Management studies.

Mid-West University follows a semester system-based curriculum. Undergraduate programs consist of four-year courses spread across eight semesters, while graduate programs are two-year courses extended over four semesters.

Situated in Birendranagar, Surkhet, the university is a public institution that was formerly the headquarters of the Mid-western development region of Nepal. It operates as an autonomous and publicly funded higher education institution.

The establishment of Mid-West University represents the realization of a cherished dream for the people of the Mid-Western Region. Although the university is still in its developmental phase, it enjoys the enthusiastic participation and support of the local community.

The University Senate (US) serves as the highest governing and decision-making body of the institution. Its responsibilities include formulating and approving university policies, providing guidance to the university and its bodies, evaluating academic programs, awarding degrees, determining the number of faculty and staff, and examining budget proposals and audit reports.

The Senate consists of various members, with the Chancellor of the University (the Prime Minister of Nepal) serving as the Chairperson and the Pro-Chancellor (the Minister of Education) as the Vice Chairperson. The Vice Chancellor, Registrar, Deans, academic faculty representatives, ex-officio members, educationists, student organization representatives, businesspersons, donors, and trustees also participate in the Senate.

Mid-West University employs a variety of teaching pedagogies, including lecture cum interactive discussions, problem-solving and case study methods, project-based learning, group discussions, report writing, field visits, guest professors, student exchange programs, reflective learning, and practical exposure in relevant industries and information technology.

Overall, Mid-West University aims to provide quality higher education and contribute to the educational and intellectual development of Nepal and the global community.

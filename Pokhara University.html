Pokhara University

Pokhara University (PU or PoU) is Nepal's fifth university, established in 1997 (2054 B.S.). It was formed along with Purbanchal University as part of a government initiative to improve access to higher education. The central office of Pokhara University is located in Pokhara, Kaski District, Gandaki Province. The university chancellor is the Prime Minister of Nepal, and the pro-chancellor is the Minister of Education. The vice chancellor serves as the principal administrator of the university.

The main campus of Pokhara University is situated in Khudi-Dhungepatan, Pokhara Lekhnath Municipality, Kaski District, approximately 13 km east of Pokhara city (Prithvi Chowk). The university also operates an academic complex in Seven Lake City, Lekhnath, surrounded by the majestic peaks of the Himalayan mountain range. The central office of the university is within walking distance of Begnas Lake and Rupa Lake.

The School of Engineering, The School of Health & Allied Sciences, The School of Business, and The School of Development and Social Engineering are located along the bank of the Khudi river.

Under the Pokhara University Act of 1997, Pokhara University began offering degree programs. It has four Constituent Schools where Bachelor's, Master's, and PhD degree programs are offered. Additionally, there are 57 academic institutions affiliated with PU, providing Bachelor's, Master's, M.Phil., and PhD degree programs.

Pokhara University follows a semester system. Bachelor's degrees span four years (eight semesters), while master's degrees require an additional two years (four semesters). The Philosophy degree program lasts for one and a half years (three semesters). All programs at the university are conducted in English.

The faculties at Pokhara University include:

1. Faculty of Sciences and Technology
2. Faculty of Management Studies
3. Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences
4. Faculty of Health Sciences

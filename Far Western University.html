Far Western University

Far-Western University, established in 2010 A.D. (2067 B.S.), is a distinguished educational institution located in Mahendranagar, Kanchanpur, Nepal. It was founded under the Act of the Constituent Assembly by the Government of Nepal. The university commenced its administrative operations in 2011 A.D. following the appointment of university officials. The Chancellor of the university is the Prime Minister of Nepal.

Far-Western University (FU) introduced its undergraduate programs in December 2012 at Siddhanath Multiple Campus (SMC). These programs cover a diverse range of subjects and are offered under the faculties of management, education, humanities and social sciences, and science and technology (computer science). The undergraduate programs follow a semester system, except for the Bachelor of Business Studies (BBS) program, which still follows the yearly system. The university is also launching graduate programs in eight different subjects in the current academic session.

Scholarships and freeships are provided by Far-Western University to deserving and meritorious students. Special provisions are made for students from socially excluded populations such as Dalits, freed Kamaiyas, freed Haliya, Janajatis, disabled students, girl students, and other underprivileged students. The criteria and number of scholarships are determined by the respective Dean's office, ensuring transparency in the selection process.

Far-Western University currently comprises six faculties, each headed by a Dean. They are as follows:

  1. Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences
  2. Faculty of Management
  3. Faculty of Education
  4. Faculty of Science and Technology
  5. Faculty of Engineering
  6. Faculty of Agriculture Science

Far-Western University is committed to providing quality education and promoting academic excellence in various disciplines. It continues to contribute significantly to the educational landscape of Nepal, fostering intellectual growth and nurturing the talents of its students.

Bachelor of Business Administration

The Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) aims at educating students and preparing them for handling managerial positions to start a professional career in business or public management. It will enable them to take over responsible positions in domestic and global business organizations with modern impersonal skills and technical know-how of the recent trends in global business.

It is designed to provide students with a better and broader perspective of the world, society, business, and life by combining the study of social sciences and humanities with the study of business and management. The curriculum is built on four targeted learning competencies for starting a career in management and pursuing advanced management knowledge.

This BBA program will develop positive attitudes, abilities, and practical skills that are needed for a competent and responsible manager, and the program will work as a foundation for master's studies in management. Currently, 48+ Business School offers BBA program in affiliation with Tribhuvan University in Nepal.

ELIGIBILITY

The candidate applying for admission to the BBA program of Tribhuvan University must have:

  1. Minimum D+ grade in each subject of grade 11 and 12 with CGPA 1.80 or more / Secured at least second division in the 10+2, PCL or equivalent program.
  2. Successfully completed twelve-year schooling or its equivalent from any university, board, or institution recognized by Tribhuvan University.
  3. Complied with all the application procedures.

Eligible applicants are required to appear in the entrance test commonly known as Central Management Admission Test (CMAT) conducted by the Faculty of Management. The test will follow the international testing pattern and standards. It includes the areas like:

  1. Verbal ability
  2. Quantitative ability
  3. Logical reasoning
  4. General awareness

There shall be altogether one hundred (100) objective questions in the CMAT containing twenty-five (25) questions in each section with a total weight of 100 marks. Students must secure a minimum of 40% in the CMAT in order to qualify for the interview.

Interview Applicants securing the above cut-off point marks in the CMAT will be short-listed. Only short-listed candidates will be interviewed and selected for admission.

"In Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA), each campus/college admits students based on the number of seats allocated per section. To be eligible for admission, students must participate in the Common Management Admission Test (CMAT). The campus/college is required to announce a mandatory application for eligible students, and they will provide a scholarship of 50% of the total amount to two students per section. Selection of students for the scholarship will be based on the priority given in the following order:

  1. Students who have passed the entrance examination from the community school and intermediate level from the community college.
  2. Students who have passed both levels from the community college.
  3. Students who have passed the entrance examination from the community school."

CURRICULAR STRUCTURE

The curricular structure of Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) programs in Nepal may vary slightly among different universities and colleges. However, I can provide you with a general outline of the BBA curriculum commonly followed in Nepal. Here is an example of the typical curricular structure:

Foundation Courses:

  • English
  • Nepali
  • Mathematics
  • Computer Application
  • Basic Economics
  • Business Communication Skills

Core Courses:

  • Principles of Management
  • Financial Accounting
  • Cost and Management Accounting
  • Business Statistics
  • Microeconomics
  • Macroeconomics
  • Business Mathematics and Statistics
  • Business Law
  • Organizational Behavior
  • Business Ethics
  • Operations Management
  • Marketing Management
  • Human Resource Management
  • Financial Management
  • Entrepreneurship and Small Business Management
  • Strategic Management

Specialization Courses:

BBA programs often offer specialization options in areas such as:

  • Finance
  • Marketing
  • Human Resource Management
  • International Business
  • Information Technology

Elective Courses:

Students may have the option to choose elective courses based on their interests and career goals. These courses can be from various business disciplines or other related fields.

Internship/Project Work:

BBA programs typically include an internship or project work component, where students gain practical experience in a real business setting and apply their theoretical knowledge.

Capstone Course:

Towards the end of the program, students may be required to complete a capstone course or a research project, which integrates their learning and demonstrates their understanding of business concepts.

It's important to note that the specific courses and their order can vary depending on the institution. Additionally, the total credit hours required to complete a BBA program in Nepal typically range from around 120 to 150 credits, which are spread across several semesters.

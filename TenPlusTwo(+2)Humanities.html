Ten Plus Two (+2) Humanities

Plus Two in Humanities is a foundation-level course designed for students who want to pursue their undergraduate studies in Mass Communication, Sociology, English, and other related fields of interest. Under the National Examination Board (NEB), the Humanities and Social Sciences stream is a two-year plus two-level foundation course. It offers a diverse range of subjects such as Language studies, Mass Communication, Sociology, Psychology, Economics, Marketing/Business Mathematics, Contemporary Society, and more. By exploring these subjects, students gain a holistic approach to education.

However, the Humanities and Social Sciences stream has been experiencing a decline in enrollment. Future job prospects and personal interests of students may have contributed to the Humanities faculty being the least preferred stream among SEE graduates.

Prof. Dr. Bidyanath Koirala, an education expert, once said, "Humanities faculty doesn't know how to sell itself." To attract present-day students, the course should be designed to reflect the present times while preserving its rich history.

ELIGIBILITY

Students must have completed SEE or an equivalent examination with a minimum GPA of 1.6. They should have at least a D+ grade in English, Nepali, and Social Studies.

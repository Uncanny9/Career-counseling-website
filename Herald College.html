Herald College

Herald College Kathmandu (HCK) offers top international degree programs in collaboration with the University of Wolverhampton in the UK. Situated in the heart of Nepal, HCK provides students with the opportunity to pursue the BSc (Hons) International Business Management, BSc (Hons) Computer Science, and International Master of Business Administration (IMBA) degrees.

Herald College Kathmandu (HCK) is committed to delivering globally recognized degrees at an affordable price. The college's experienced faculty members provide guidance, inspiration, and valuable training to equip students with the skills needed to thrive in the job market. HCK offers a skill-based learning environment, including industry visits and internship programs, to provide students with a global platform. The teaching methods and academic environment at HCK are designed to mirror those in the UK, promoting practical education and empowering students to make a positive impact on their future.

One of the key features of HCK is its direct and full-time UK university courses. Through its close collaboration with the University of Wolverhampton, HCK offers bachelor's degrees in IT and business. These courses are developed, approved, and taught by the University of Wolverhampton in partnership with New Zealand. Students at HCK have the opportunity to complete their entire bachelor's degree without the need for credit transfer. They receive full undergraduate status in the UK, enhancing the value and recognition of their degree.

Herald College Kathmandu is operated by a team of pioneers in UK education, combining the expertise of local and foreign academics. As one of the earliest institutions to offer UK education in Nepal, HCK has a long history of collaboration with a UK university. This experience, dating back to the establishment of Islington Institution in 1996, has resulted in over 3500 graduates.

At HCK, the British style of learning is implemented, which emphasizes the lecture-tutorial-workshop teaching method commonly used in the UK. Rather than being solely knowledge-based, the courses focus on fostering self-study, research, leadership, and entrepreneurship. By challenging students to think creatively, HCK prepares them to excel in their chosen fields.

International exposure is an integral part of the educational experience at Herald College Kathmandu. Students have the opportunity to participate in the University of Wolverhampton's 15-Day Summer Camp program in the UK, fully funded by the college. This program enables students to interact with peers from around the world, broadening their global perspective.

HCK provides excellent academic and social amenities to ensure students receive an exceptional education and enjoy their studies. Access to outstanding resources and facilities enhances the learning experience, and students benefit from being part of a reputable university community.

Students at Herald College Kathmandu learn from highly qualified and internationally renowned faculty members. These faculty members bring a wealth of academic and professional experience, applying theoretical concepts to real-world situations. Their connections and networks provide students with valuable opportunities for employment and further career development.

For admission, students who have completed or are in the process of completing their upper secondary final exams can apply directly to the university. Required documents include academic transcripts, passports or citizenship certificates, and birth certificates or citizenship information of either parent. Provisional admission may be granted to students awaiting their exam results. The admissions staff at HCK guide students through the application process and discuss the entry requirements upon arrival. The minimum age requirement for applicants is 18 years old.

Herald College Kathmandu (HCK) offers a range of courses in collaboration with the University of Wolverhampton in the UK. The available courses at HCK include:

1. BSc (Hons) International Business Management: This program focuses on developing students' understanding of global business practices, international trade, marketing strategies, and management principles. Students gain knowledge and skills necessary for managing businesses in the global marketplace.

2. BSc (Hons) Computer Science: This course provides students with a comprehensive understanding of computer science and its applications. Students learn programming languages, software development, database management, computer networks, and cybersecurity.

3. International Master of Business Administration (IMBA): The IMBA program at HCK is designed for professionals seeking to enhance their business acumen and leadership skills. It covers a wide range of business areas, including finance, marketing, human resource management, and strategic management.

These courses are fully developed and taught by the University of Wolverhampton, ensuring a high-quality education and international recognition. HCK offers students the opportunity to pursue these UK degree programs in Nepal, providing them with a solid foundation for their careers in business, technology, and management.

Bachelor of Architecture

The Bachelor program in Architecture, leading to B.Arch. (Bachelor of Architecture) degree is a five-year course that aims to provide systematic knowledge and skills in the planning, design, and conservation of buildings and other physical artifacts with concerns for aesthetics, comfort, durability, social responsiveness, and integration with natural and man-made landscapes. The area of instruction covers a spectrum of courses relating to planning, structural design, landscape management, interior design and furnishing, and conservation and restoration of application of electronic devices and their applications in arrays of communication applications.

ELIGIBILITY

  • The candidates should have completed Class 12 examination of NEB or equivalent level from a recognized academic institution.
  • The candidates should have studied at least one course (having at least 100 full marks or 5 credits) each in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • The candidates should have scored a minimum of 45% or a CGPA/GPA of 2.0 (in a scale of 4.0) and at least Grade C (in case of A-level, at least Grade D) or 45% in each subject (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics).
  • The minimum criterion of Grade C (in case of A level, Grade D) or 45% does not apply to other courses or extra courses, if any. Candidates should have studied mathematics of at least 100 marks in Class 11 or class 12 examination of NEB or equivalent level.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

Here is the curricular structure for the Bachelor in Architecture program, organized by semester, subject, credit hour, and code:

1st Semester:

  • Mathematics I (3 hrs, MTH 112)
  • Building Materials (2 hrs, ARC 151)
  • Architecture Modeling (2 hrs, ARC 111)
  • Architectural Graphics I (2 hrs, ARC 112)
  • Introduction to Architecture (2 hrs, ARC 121)
  • Basic Design I (4 hrs, ARC 101)

2nd Semester:

  • Mathematics II (2 hrs, MTH 122)
  • Communication Technique (2 hrs, Eng 104)
  • Computer and Programming (2 hrs, CMP 101)
  • Architectural Graphics II (2 hrs, ARC 113)
  • Photography (2 hrs, ARC 114)
  • Basic Design II (4 hrs, ARC 102)

3rd Semester:

  • Structure Forms (2 hrs, ARC 222)
  • Surveying (2 hrs, SRV 202)
  • Building Science I (2 hrs, ARC 241)
  • Visual Arts (2 hrs, ARC 215)
  • History of Western Arch (2 hrs, ARC 231)
  • Architectural Design I (4 hrs, ARC 203)

4th Semester:

  • Statics and Dynamics (2 hrs, MEC 208)
  • Computer Aided Design I (2 hrs, CMP 212)
  • Building Services I (2 hrs, ARC 242)
  • Building Construction I (2 hrs, ARC 252)
  • History of Eastern Arch (2 hrs, ARC 232)
  • Architectural Design II (5 hrs, ARC 204)

5th Semester:

  • Structure I (2 hrs, STR 331)
  • Computer Aided Design II (2 hrs, CMP 313)
  • Building Construction II (2 hrs, ARC 353)
  • Architectural Elective I (2 hrs, ARC 361)
  • History of Nepalese Arch (2 hrs, ARC 333)
  • Architectural Design III (5 hrs, ARC 305)

6th Semester:

  • Structure II (3 hrs, STR 332)
  • Building Science II (2 hrs, ARC 342)
  • Building Construction III (2 hrs, ARC 354)
  • Landscape Architecture (2 hrs, ARC 371)
  • History of Modern Arch (2 hrs, ARC 334)
  • Architecture Design IV (5 hrs, ARC 306)

7th Semester:

  • Structure III (3 hrs, STR 433)
  • Settlement Planning (3 hrs, ARC 481)
  • Architecture Elective II (2 hrs, ARC 462)
  • Architecture Design V (5 hrs, ARC 407)
  • Working Drawing And Detailing (2 hrs, ARC 423)

8th Semester:

  • Practical Office Experience (15 hrs, ARC 569)

9th Semester:

  • Structure IV (2 hrs, STR 434)
  • Architecture Elective III (2 hrs, ARC 563)
  • Estimating and Valuation (2 hrs, ARC 455)
  • Directed Studies and Seminar (2 hrs, ARC 465)
  • Building Service II (2 hrs, ARC 443)
  • Architecture Design VI (5 hrs, ARC 408)

10th Semester:

  • Management And Economics (3 hrs, ARC 524)
  • Architecture And Society (2 hrs, SOC 411)
  • Architectural Design VII Thesis (10 hrs, ARC 509)

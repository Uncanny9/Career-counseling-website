M.Sc. Interdisciplinary Water Resource Management

Nepal Engineering College (NEC) is offering a two-year Master's Degree Program in Interdisciplinary Water Resources Management in affiliation with Pokhara University.

The objective of the program is to produce qualified professionals with interdisciplinary knowledge and skills and the capacity to respond to the established and emerging challenges in water resources management.

The program is partially supported under the Crossing Boundaries (CB) Project with funding from the Government of the Netherlands. The CB Project support includes 10 fellowships, called South Asian Water Alliance Fellowship (SAWA-Fellowship), awarded to 8 female and 2 male students every year.

Ten seats are also available for full-fee paying students which amounts to Rs. 200,000.00 for the entire course. Applications are invited from eligible candidates for admission to the course and for the award of the SAWA-Fellowship.

ELIGIBILITY

Minimum Bachelor's degree in Science and Technology (Engineering, Applied Sciences, Natural Sciences, or Basic Sciences) with 15 years of education completed from a recognized university or institution, securing a minimum of second division marks.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

The Master of Science in Interdisciplinary Water Resources Management Program of Pokhara University is designed to train water professionals with interdisciplinary knowledge and skills in pursuing and promoting integrated water resources management in the country, consistent with the water resources development policy set forth by the Water Resources Strategy (2002) and National Water Plan (2005).

The duration of the program is 2 years organized into 4 semesters. A student needs to successfully complete 44 credit hours of coursework and 16 credit hours of thesis for graduation.

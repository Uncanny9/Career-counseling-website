Master of Health Care Management

The Master of Health Care Management (MHCM) course is designed to meet the needs of advanced-level management practices and research as required for health care and non-health care graduates. It is a blend of advanced level theories and practices with the support of a compulsory practicum and research programs. It provides a wider spectrum of health and specific knowledge of management theory in health care management with IT backup.

The MHCM program offered by the Faculty of Management Studies (FMS) at Pokhara University is designed to provide a comprehensive education in Health Care Management. It aims to prepare students for professional advancement and managerial positions in the field by equipping them with theoretical knowledge and practical skills necessary to effectively lead and manage healthcare organizations. The program focuses on developing a positive attitude and essential abilities, including critical thinking, problem-solving, communication, and leadership skills, which are vital for competent and responsible managers. Additionally, it aims to groom students for prominent roles such as Chief of Hospital Management or Hospital Administrators in various healthcare settings. The curriculum emphasizes specialized knowledge in healthcare administration, equipping students to handle the complexities of managing healthcare facilities. Overall, the MHCM program strives to produce competent healthcare managers who can contribute to the improvement and efficiency of healthcare services, benefiting the broader community.

ELIGIBILITY

Applicants having a minimum of 15 years of formal education (12 years of schooling plus 3 years of graduation) are eligible to apply for the MHCM program. Eligible qualifications include BHCM degree or Post Graduate Diploma in HCM, Bachelor degree in health science stream, or equivalent degree with a CGPA of 2/4 (second division) or above.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

It is a two-year program spread over four semesters. The program consists of 54 credit hours comprising of core courses, professional courses, thesis, and an internship.

Here is the curricular structure for the MHCM program, organized by semester:

Semester I

  • HHM 707 Health Economics - 3 credit hours
  • HHM 705 Managed Care & Health Insurance - 3 credit hours
  • HHM 703 Recent Development in Health Care - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 636 Research Methodology - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 641 Strategic Health Care Management - 3 credit hours

Total Credits: 15

Semester II

  • HHM 709 Population Dynamics - 3 credit hours
  • HHM 701 Clinical Service Management - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 635 HRD in Health Care Organization - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 631 Health Program Management - 3 credit hours
  • HHM 634 Health Environment & Development - 3 credit hours

Total Credits: 15

Semester III

  • MIS 642 IT in Health Care - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 637 Quality Management in Health Care - 3 credit hours
  • MGT 633 Health Care Marketing - 3 credit hours
  • HHM 632 Health Care Evaluation - 3 credit hours

Total Credits: 12

Semester IV

  • HHM 700 Health Care Practicum - 6 credit hours
  • HHM 800 Thesis (Compulsory) - 6 credit hours

Total Credits: 12

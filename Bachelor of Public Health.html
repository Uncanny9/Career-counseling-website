Bachelor of Public Health

Bachelor in Public Health (BPH) is a four-year program of Pokhara University. The course aims to prepare a student with all necessary scientific knowledge, technical competencies, and human understanding to work in various capacities from the community level to the central level health care programs.

Currently, six Public Health Colleges offer the BPH Program in Nepal with affiliation from Pokhara University.

Provision of equitable access to health care for attainment of an acceptable level of health and better quality of life of the people by creating more resources is the dominant concern for Nepal today. In many parts of the world scientists are investing themselves in introducing public health interventions that can deal with the lifestyle. However, Nepal is facing four aid burdens of diseases (communicable, life-style related, poverty, and conflict). In order to cope with this complex situation, the development of public health graduates with necessary skills has become inevitable.

The Bachelor's in Public Health (BPH) program aims to produce competent graduates who are well-equipped with the necessary skills and knowledge to effectively contribute to public health programs. By the end of the course, students will have developed expertise in applied health sciences, including laboratory work and practical applications. They will also gain a strong foundation in public health, primary health, health systems development, health economics, nutrition, and environmental health. The program emphasizes training in epidemiology and disease control as well as the design, analysis, and evaluation of public health research and management. Students will also enhance their skills in human resource development and gain insights into current issues in public health management. The program focuses on developing research and scientific writing skills through term papers and a thesis. Additionally, students will gain practical experience through community diagnosis and comprehensive field practices, while also participating in extracurricular activities such as training, seminars, and exposure to enhance their overall competencies.

ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for the program, students must fulfill one of the following criteria:

1. Must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology from a recognized educational institution and passed with a minimum of 50 percent or 2.4 CGPA in the Proficiency Certificate Level or Grade 12 Science group.

2. Students from A-level or other similar programs with different grading systems must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology with a minimum of 50 percent or 2.4 CGPA.

JOB PROSPECTS

A degree in public health opens up a range of career opportunities in Nepal and abroad. As Nepal focuses on improving health status and achieving equitable access to healthcare, the demand for public health professionals is increasing. With the country facing various health burdens, including communicable diseases, lifestyle-related issues, and poverty, the need for public health graduates has become essential. Graduates with a Bachelor's in Public Health (BPH) can pursue careers in universities, health organizations, health centers, hospitals, and the Health Research Council. The field of public health offers diverse opportunities, attracting professionals from various backgrounds who specialize in areas such as education, research, administration, environmental health, social work, and more. Public health professionals play a crucial role in addressing complex health challenges, improving access to healthcare, controlling infectious diseases, and promoting well-being. Working in public health allows individuals to serve communities at the local, national, and international levels, making a positive impact on people's lives. Whether you are passionate about numbers, research, or working directly with people, public health offers a rewarding and fulfilling career where you can contribute to improving health outcomes. Public health professionals can work in both public and private sectors, including government health departments, non-profit organizations focused on health advocacy or research, and private companies in the pharmaceutical or health insurance sectors. Pursuing a degree in public health is ideal for individuals who are motivated to make a difference and are dedicated to enhancing the well-being of others.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

Curriculum of Bachelor of Public Health (BPH) of Pokhara University:

Semester I

  • Introduction to Public Health
  • Anatomy and Physiology
  • Pharmacology, Pharmacy, and Biochemistry
  • Microbiology and Immunology
  • Technical English
  • Bio-Statistic and Computer Application I
  • Laboratories and Term Papers

Semester II

  • Pathology and First Aid
  • Toxicology and Entomology
  • Bio-Statistic and Computer Application II
  • Basic Epidemiology I
  • Public Health
  • Laboratories and Term Papers

Semester III

  • Basic Epidemiology II
  • Introduction to Sociology and Anthropology
  • Applied Epidemiology I
  • Health System Development I
  • Health Promotion and Education
  • Term Papers

Semester IV

  • Applied Epidemiology II
  • Medical Sociology and Anthropology
  • Environmental and Occupational Health
  • Food and Nutrition I
  • Applied Health Promotion and Education
  • Term Papers

Semester V

  • Environmental and Occupational Health
  • Food and Nutrition II
  • Human Resource Development (HRD)
  • Primary Health Care in Nepal
  • Health System Development II
  • Term Papers

Semester VI

  • Research Methodology
  • Family Health
  • Applied Child Health
  • Applied Environmental and Occupational Health II
  • Community Diagnosis
  • Term Papers

Semester VII

  • Health Planning and Policy Development
  • Health Economics
  • Research and Computer Software Application

Semester VIII

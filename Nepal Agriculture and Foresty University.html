Nepal Agriculture and Forestry University

The Agriculture and Forestry University (AFU) is a public agricultural university located in Rampur, Chitwan, Nepal. It was established in June 2010 by the Parliament of Nepal through a bill that merged two constituent campuses of Tribhuvan University: the Rampur Agriculture Campus of the Institute of Agriculture and Animal Science, and the Forestry Campus in Hetauda, Makwanpur of the Institute of Forestry.

AFU aims to develop a skilled agricultural workforce and promote research in agriculture, forestry, and related disciplines through its teaching, research, and extension programs across the country. The university plays a vital role in producing manpower for the agriculture and forestry industries.

The central office and the University Complex of AFU are located in Rampur, Chitwan. The university has a well-defined organizational structure, academic programs, and curricula that have been developed by experienced professionals in the field. These programs are based on extensive reviews of leading Agriculture and Forestry universities in the SAARC region, Southeast Asia, and developed countries.

As the first university in Nepal based on the Land Grant Model, AFU is responsible for conducting instruction, research, and extension programs to support the development of agriculture and forestry in the country.

The faculties of Agriculture, Animal Sciences, Veterinary, and Fisheries provide undergraduate degrees such as BSc in Agriculture (4 years), B.V.Sc. and A.H (5 years), BSc in Fisheries (4 years), as well as two-year master's programs and PhD programs in Agriculture, Animal Science, Veterinary Science, and Aquaculture. The Faculty of Forestry offers a Bachelor of Forestry degree, a Master of Forestry degree, and a PhD program.

The university has a faculty strength of 140 members at the Rampur campus and 55 members at the Faculty of Forestry in Hetauda. It serves approximately 1,500 undergraduate students, 200 postgraduate students, and around 50 PhD scholars in the fields of agriculture and forestry. The Rampur campus covers an area of 280 hectares (690 acres), while the Hetauda Campus spans 95 hectares (230 acres).

The faculty members are engaged in research activities focused on agricultural productivity, post-harvest technology, food product preservation, biodiversity conservation, and enhancing livelihood systems and innovation capacities of farming communities. The postgraduate students contribute to research through their thesis work, generating knowledge and technologies to address the challenges in agriculture and forestry in Nepal.


The Agriculture and Forestry University (AFU) in Nepal offers a range of courses in the field of agriculture, animal sciences, veterinary sciences, fisheries, and forestry. Here are some of the available courses:

Faculty of Agriculture:
- Bachelor of Science in Agriculture (B.Sc. Ag)
- Master of Science in Agriculture (M.Sc. Ag)
- PhD in Agriculture

Faculty of Animal Sciences, Veterinary, and Fisheries:
- Bachelor of Veterinary Science and Animal Husbandry (B.V.Sc. & A.H.)
- Bachelor of Science in Fisheries (B.Sc. Fisheries)
- Master of Science in Animal Science (M.Sc. Animal Science)
- Master of Science in Veterinary Science (M.Sc. Veterinary Science)
- Master of Science in Aquaculture (M.Sc. Aquaculture)
- PhD in Animal Science
- PhD in Veterinary Science
- PhD in Fisheries

Faculty of Forestry:
- Bachelor of Science in Forestry (B.Sc. Forestry)
- Master of Science in Forestry (M.Sc. Forestry)
- PhD in Forestry

These courses cover various aspects of agriculture, animal sciences, veterinary sciences, fisheries, and forestry, providing students with theoretical knowledge, practical skills, and research opportunities in their respective fields of study. The university aims to produce skilled professionals who can contribute to the development and sustainability of the agricultural and forestry sectors in Nepal.

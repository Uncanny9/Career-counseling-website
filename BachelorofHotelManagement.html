Bachelor of Hotel Management

The Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) program of Tribhuvan University has been designed to prepare students to enter the hospitality industry at the supervisory level in the areas of hotel and catering operations.

The BHM program of Tribhuvan University is a four-year, eight-semester program offered in more than 10 Hotel Management colleges in Nepal. Every year, the total capacity of enrollment for the BHM program of Tribhuvan University is more than 600.

ELIGIBILITY

The candidates applying for admission to the Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) program must have:

  • Students who have passed 10+2 or equivalent examinations from recognized academic institutes with a second division (securing 45% and above) or 1.8 CGPA are eligible to enroll in this program.
  • Must have studied English as a compulsory subject.

ADMISSION PROCEDURES

To apply for admission to the Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) course, candidates must complete and submit the designated application form for the entrance test within the specified timeframe. Along with the application form, applicants are required to include attested copies of their academic certificates and testimonials from all previously completed examinations. Additionally, they should provide equivalency, transfer, and character certificates, as well as two recent passport-sized photographs. It is necessary for candidates to have obtained a minimum of 40% marks in the CMAT (Common Management Admission Test) conducted by Tribhuvan University (T.U.). These requirements ensure that candidates meet the necessary qualifications for consideration for admission into the BHM program.

CURRICULAR STRUCTURE

The curricular structure of the Bachelor of Hotel Management (BHM) program in Nepal may vary slightly among different universities and colleges. However, I can provide you with a general outline of the BHM curriculum commonly followed in Nepal. Here is an example of the typical curricular structure:

  1. Foundation Courses:
    • English
    • Nepali
    • Mathematics
    • Computer Application
    • Basic Accounting
  2. Core Courses:
    • Introduction to Hospitality and Tourism
    • Food and Beverage Production
    • Food and Beverage Service
    • Front Office Management
    • Housekeeping Management
    • Hotel Operations Management
    • Principles of Management
    • Principles of Marketing
    • Human Resource Management
    • Financial Management
    • Business Communication
    • Food Safety and Hygiene
    • Hospitality Law and Ethics
    • Hospitality Information Systems
    • Event Management
    • Facilities Management
    • Customer Relationship Management
  3. Specialization Courses:
    • BHM programs often offer specialization options in areas such as:
    • Culinary Arts and Kitchen Management
    • Food and Beverage Service Management
    • Front Office Operations and Management
    • Housekeeping Operations and Management
    • Event Management
    • Hotel Sales and Marketing
  4. Elective Courses:
    • Students may have the option to choose elective courses based on their interests and career goals. These courses can be from various hospitality or management disciplines.
  5. Internship/Industrial Training:
    • BHM programs typically include an internship or industrial training component, where students gain practical experience in a real hotel or hospitality setting and apply their theoretical knowledge.
  6. Capstone Project:
    • Towards the end of the program, students may be required to complete a capstone project, which integrates their learning and demonstrates their understanding of hotel management concepts.

It's important to note that the specific courses and their order can vary depending on the institution. Additionally, the total credit hours required to complete a BHM program in Nepal typically range from around 120 to 150 credits, which are spread across several semesters.

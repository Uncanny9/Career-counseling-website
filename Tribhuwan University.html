Tribhuwan University

Tribhuvan University, also known as TU, is a public university located in Kirtipur, Kathmandu. Established in 1959, TU is the oldest and largest university in Nepal, with approximately 3.5 lakh students. The university offers a wide range of academic programs, including 1000 undergraduate and 500 postgraduate courses across various disciplines. It has 62 constituent campuses and over 1080 affiliated colleges spread throughout the country.

TU was named after the late King Tribhuvan and initially operated from Tripureshwor Campus. In 1967, the university relocated to its main campus in Kirtipur, which spans over 154.77 hectares and houses the Central Administrative Office and the Central Campus. Over the years, TU has expanded its programs and faculties, now offering 115 courses at the technical proficiency certificate level, 1079 courses at the Bachelor's level, and 1000 courses at the Master's level. It also offers PhD degrees in technical institutes and faculties.

To accommodate the growing number of students, TU started affiliating private colleges in 1979, leading to the establishment of numerous colleges in the private sector. As of April 2016, 1,084 private and public colleges were affiliated with TU. In the 2014-2015 academic session, TU had a total enrollment of 405,341 students, with 148,141 studying in constituent campuses and 257,200 in affiliated colleges.

TU is proud of its faculty, consisting of 7,841 teaching staff and 7,413 non-teaching staff across its campuses. It has been ranked among the top 800-1000 universities globally and among the 251-300 best universities in Asia according to the Times Higher Education World University Rankings and the 4icu Organisation of University Ranking, respectively.

The university operates several technical institutes, including the Institute of Agriculture and Animal Science, Institute of Engineering, Institute of Forestry, Institute of Medicine, and Institute of Science and Technology. Each institute specializes in specific domains within the field of science and technology education.

TU comprises four faculties and 40 associated central departments, offering a diverse range of academic disciplines. It has 62 constituent campuses, and a partial list of colleges is available. As a government-financed institution, TU enjoys autonomy, with the prime minister serving as the chancellor and the Minister of Education as the pro-chancellor. The current vice-chancellor is Prof. Dr. Dharma Kant Baskota, and the registrar is Prof. Dr. Peshal Dahal.

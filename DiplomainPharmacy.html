Diploma in Pharmacy

Pharmacy or Pharmacology is one of the main disciplines under Biomedical Science and the pharmaceutical industry. It is the profession responsible for the preparation, dispensing, and appropriate use of medication to achieve optimal therapeutic outcomes. Pharmacy is a scientific profession that requires a high level of education and training.

Pharmacy degrees, including the Diploma in Pharmacy (Pharmacy Assistant level), are approved by the Nepal Pharmacy Council (NPC) to ensure that students receive appropriate education for their professional registration and subsequent development in their careers.

The Nepal Pharmacy Council has established guidelines for the accreditation of Pharmacy degree programs and the establishment of new Pharmacy degree colleges. There are 21 colleges offering a three-year Diploma Level (Pharmacy Assistant level) program under the affiliation of the Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training (CTEVT). The entry requirement for this level is the completion of 10 years of formal schooling.

Diploma in Pharmacy is a three-year course after the Secondary Education Examination (SEE). Students who have passed the SEE with a minimum aggregate score of 45% and have an average GPA of at least 2 can join the course.

ELIGIBILITY

Secured at least grade C in Mathematics, English, and Science in the School Leaving Certificate (SLC) examination from a recognized academic institution, and an average GPA of at least 2.

JOB PROSPECTS

A Diploma in Pharmacy graduate, known as a pharmacy assistant as per the Pharmacy Council Act 2057, has various career opportunities, including:

1. Continuing education in different universities in the country and abroad.
2. Opening retail pharmacies in municipality areas.
3. Employment in government and private hospital pharmacies.
4. Working in community pharmacies, drug manufacturing industries, and different health-related NGOs and INGOs.
5. Working as instructors in Pharmacy educational institutions.

ADMISSION CRITERIA

Passed the entrance examination organized by CTEVT.

Applicants should submit the following documents at the time of application:

1. SLC pass certificate or equivalent certificate
2. Character certificate
3. Citizenship certificate (for name, parents' name, age, date of birth, and address verification purposes only)
4. Medical fitness certificate (at the time of admission)

Note: Student quotas for different categories are as per the policy of the Nepal Pharmacy Council and CTEVT.

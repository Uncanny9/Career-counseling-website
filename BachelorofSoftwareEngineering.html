Bachelor of Software Engineering

The Bachelor of Software Engineering program is a 4-year (8 semesters) undergraduate engineering program with 133 credits. Graduates of this program possess knowledge and skills in complex systems analysis, planning, design, and construction. The program follows a unique, project-driven curriculum that fosters communication, teamwork, critical thinking, and professionalism.

The goal of the program is to provide a professionally guided education in software engineering that prepares graduates for a broad range of career options in industry, government, computing graduate programs, and professional education.

Software engineering is a young and challenging discipline that continues to evolve as information technologies open up new areas of exploration. Software engineers combine theory and practice from computer science, engineering, and traditional IT disciplines to create reliable and efficient software that works on real machines.

ELIGIBILITY

  • The candidates should have completed Class 12 examination of NEB or an equivalent level from a recognized academic institution.
  • The candidates should have studied at least one course (with at least 100 full marks or 5 credits) each in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
  • The candidates should have scored a minimum of 45% or a CGPA/GPA of 2.0 (on a scale of 4.0) and at least Grade C (in case of A-level, at least Grade D) or 45% in each subject (Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics).
  • The minimum criterion of Grade C (in case of A level, Grade D) or 45% does not apply to other courses or extra courses. However, candidates should have studied mathematics of at least 100 marks in Class 11 or Class 12 examination of NEB or an equivalent level.

JOB PROSPECTS

The Bachelor of Engineering (BE) program in Software Engineering is designed to meet the growing demand for skilled software engineers in the industry. Graduates of this program are equipped with the expertise required for large-scale software systems projects, making them highly sought after by businesses and government organizations involved in extensive software development. Additionally, their skills are equally valuable for smaller projects and businesses offering software engineering and integration services.

Upon completion of the program, students are well-prepared for diverse career paths such as applications developers, internet developers, programming specialists, systems programmers, software designers, engineers, architects, consultants, and developers. The program's focus on software development ensures graduates have ample opportunities in applications development, systems development, and computer networks. By providing a comprehensive curriculum, the program aims to train highly technical and skilled programmers who can compete with graduates from IT and computer science backgrounds, thereby contributing to the advancement of technology across industries, government organizations, and enterprises.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

The curriculum of BE Software Engineering consists of 133 credit hours to be completed in four years. The course descriptions are as follows:

1st Semester:

  • Engineering Mathematics-I
  • Physics
  • Communication Technique
  • Problem Solving Techniques
  • Fundamentals of IT
  • Programming in C

2nd Semester:

  • Engineering Mathematics-II
  • Logic Circuits
  • Mathematical Foundation of Computer Science
  • Engineering Drawing
  • Object-Oriented Programming in C++
  • Web Technology

3rd Semester:

  • Engineering Mathematics-III
  • Software Engineering Fundamentals
  • Microprocessor & Assembly Lang. Pro.
  • Data Structure and Algorithms
  • Probability & Queuing Theory
  • Programming in Java
  • Project I

4th Semester:

  • Numerical Methods
  • Computer Graphics
  • Computer Organization & Architecture
  • Database Management Systems
  • Object-Oriented Design & Modeling through UML
  • Project II

5th Semester:

  • Applied Operating System
  • Simulation & Modeling
  • Artificial Intelligence & Neural Network
  • System Programming
  • Analysis & Design of Algorithm
  • Organization and Management
  • Project II

6th Semester:

  • Multimedia Systems
  • Computer Networks
  • Principles of Programming Languages
  • Engineering Economics
  • Object-Oriented Software Development
  • Project II

7th Semester:

  • Real-Time Systems
  • Distributed Systems
  • Enterprise Application Development
  • Image Processing and Pattern Recognition
  • Software Testing, Verification, Validation, and Quality Assurance
  • Elective II

8th Semester:

  • Network Programming
  • Software Project Management
  • Elective II
  • Major Project

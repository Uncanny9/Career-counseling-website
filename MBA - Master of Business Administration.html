MBA - Master of Business Administration

The Master of Business Administration (MBA) program at Pokhara University is a general management degree. The MBA degree represents career collaboration and personal developments that are intrinsically linked to achieving career aspirations. For some students, the Pokhara University MBA is the next step on their existing career trajectory. For others, it provides the opportunity to reconsider their futures and either embark on a related field or a totally new career direction.

The MBA program is designed to bring corporate change in Nepal through the transformation of students into competent managers, executives, and entrepreneurs capable of becoming strategic change agents in the corporate and social world. The program focuses on developing a social and developmental outlook and adequate skills in analysis, decision-making, implementation, leadership, and communication among the students. The minimum duration of the degree is 24 months spread over 6 trimesters. For successful completion of the degree, a student is required to earn 66 credit hours by successfully completing 27 courses, 4 practicums, and graduate seminars.

ELIGIBILITY

Anyone having a bachelor's degree or equivalent with 15 years of formal education (12 years of schooling and three years of graduation) in any discipline with a minimum second division in the undergraduate level can apply for admission to the MBA program.

JOB PROSPECTS

This degree offers various career prospects for graduates. Some of the job sectors for MBA graduates include:

  • Ambulatory Care Pharmacist
  • Consultant Pharmacy
  • Government drug store-Armed Services
  • Government drug store-Public Health
  • Healing facility and Institutional Pharmacy
  • Managed Care Pharmacy
  • Pharmaceutical Sciences

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

The MBA program consists of a total of 66 credit hours, including foundation courses, analytical tool courses, core and functional area courses, integrative courses, concentration and elective courses. The program also includes two thematic graduate seminars and three workshops. Students also undertake an internship project during the fifth trimester. The regular MBA program is to be completed within six trimesters, with each trimester lasting 12 weeks excluding examinations. The minimum time period to complete the program is 22 months (six trimesters), and the maximum time of completion is four years from the date of registration.

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS)

The Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) is a four-and-a-half-year integrated program followed by a compulsory one-year internship training at Tribhuvan University.

Eight Medical Colleges in Nepal offer the MBBS program with affiliation from Tribhuvan University.

The standards and criteria of the course strictly adhere to the requirements of the Nepal Medical Council. The first two academic years focus on basic medical science with interdepartmental integration, as well as clinical and community correlation. A holistic approach to health and the healthcare system in Nepal is taken.

ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for the program, students must have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology from a recognized educational institution and passed with a minimum of 50 percent or a CGPA of 2.4 in the proficiency certificate level or Grade 12 Science group.

Alternatively, students from A-level or other similar programs with different grading systems should have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Biology subjects and passed with a minimum of 50 percent or a CGPA of 2.4.

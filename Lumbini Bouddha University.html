Lumbini Bouddha University

Lumbini Buddhist University, located in Lumbini, Nepal, the birthplace of the Buddha, is a tertiary educational institution. The university's inception was inspired by the First World Buddhist Summit held in Lumbini in 1998, and it was officially established on 17 June 2004. The Lumbini Buddhist University Act, enacted on 10 November 2006, solidified its legal status and ensured financial support from the government of Nepal.

The university offers a range of academic programs, including a four-year Bachelor's degree in Buddhism, as well as Master's and PhD courses.

Lumbini Buddhist University, situated in Lumbini, the sacred birthplace of Lord Gautama Buddha, is a fully-fledged university established under the Parliament Act 2004 by the Government of Nepal. In addition to the central college in Lumbini, there are affiliated colleges such as the Lotus Buddhist Academic College in Lalitpur, Boudha Multiple Campus, Lumbini College of Buddhism and Himalayan Studies, Saugat Baudha Mahavidyalaya, Jiri Buddhist College, Theravada Buddhist Academy Kathmandu, Sowa Rigpa International College, and Lumbini International Academy of Science and Technology under the umbrella of Lumbini Buddhist University. The Vice Chancellor of Lumbini Buddhist University is Prof. Dr. Hridaya Ratna Bajracharya. Presently, the university employs 200 academic staff members and 40 administrative staff members.

The academic programs at Lumbini Buddhist University are organized into the following faculties:

  1. Faculty of Buddhist Studies
  2. Faculty of Humanities and Social Science

The Faculty of Buddhist Studies is inspired by the teachings of Lord Gautama Buddha and his message of peace and harmony. Within this faculty, there are various academic disciplines offered at the Bachelor's and Master's levels. The primary academic programs include:

  • Bachelor's program in Bhot Buddhist studies
  • Master's Degree in Mahayan Buddhism
  • Master's in Buddhism and Himalayan Studies
  • Master's in Theravada Buddhism
  • Master's in Buddhism and Peace Studies
  • Postgraduate Diploma (PGD) in Archaeology and Buddhist Archaeological
  • Master's in AgroForestry
  • Master's in Buddhism and Himalayan Studies
  • PGD in Library, Information Science, and Buddhist Archival Management
  • Master's in Buddhist Studies
  • PGD in Yogic Science and Buddhism
  • PhD in Buddhist studies
  • Master's in Archaeology and Buddhist Archaeological Science

Under the Faculty of Humanities and Social Science, specifically the School of Development Studies and Applied Science, the following programs are offered:

  • Master's in Agroforestry
  • Master's in Development Studies
  • Master's in Environmental Studies

The university also provides certification programs in the following languages:

  • Certificate in Pali Language
  • Certificate in Sanskrit Language
  • Certificate in Chinese Language
  • Certificate in Tibetan Language

The organizational structure of Lumbini Buddhist University includes the following positions:

  • Chancellor: Prime Minister of Nepal (Sher Bahadur Deuba)
  • Pro-Chancellor: Minister of Education (Devendra Poudel)
  • Vice-Chancellor: Prof. Dr. Hridaya Ratna Bajracharya
  • Registrar: Dr. Tilak Ram Acharya
  • Dean: Dr. Manik Ratna Shakya

In addition to the mainstream academic programs offering Bachelor's, Master's, and Doctorate degrees, Lumbini Buddhist University houses the Lumbini Research Centre. This center promotes Buddhist principles of compassion, peace, and sustainable development. It conducts academic research in various fields, including Buddhist philosophy, literature, education, culture, development studies, applied science, and current social issues. The aim is to provide insights and foresights in areas such as management, arts, social sciences, literature, and national security.

Bachelor of Business Administration in Banking and Insurance

The BBA-BI is a four-year, 8-semester bachelor's degree program run by Pokhara University through various affiliated Business Colleges. Currently, 16 Business Schools offer the BBA-BI program in Nepal.

A student needs to successfully complete 126 credit hours of coursework, a summer project, and an internship before graduation. The course is designed to accelerate professional development in students with superior career orientation and versatility.

Over the last two decades, the field of banking, finance, and insurance has become increasingly technical, specialized, and competitive. Innovations in IT have revolutionized this industry, and the entry of global players in this industry has made the market even more competitive. Banking and insurance organizations now require employees who can exhibit skills, knowledge, and experience beyond those provided by typical traditional academic institutes. As a result, there is a significant demand for academic courses that specifically focus on the concepts and skills required for the banking and insurance business. The existing courses in the country do not fully cater to this emerging demand.

The internship lasts for a period of six to eight weeks. Colleges arrange the internship for all the students enrolled in the program. During the internship, a student is supervised by a faculty supervisor assigned from the college and a supervisor from the respective organization where he/she is doing the internship.


ELIGIBILITY

To be eligible for the BBA-BI program, a student must:

  • Pass Grade 12 examination with a minimum CGPA of 1.8
  • Achieve a minimum of D+ grade in every subject in Grade 11 and 12

JOB PROSPECTS

The banking and insurance sectors in the country and abroad are growing rapidly. The BBA-BI course prepares students to join at entry-level positions in any banking, finance, and insurance organization. Since they are educated and trained in the conceptual and operational aspects of these sectors, BBA-BI graduates are better placed to work for such organizations and can prove to be more competent professionals. Besides these organizations, graduates can also work in the functional areas of any organization, whether private or public, domestic or international.


CURRICULAR STRUCTURE

The curricular structure of the Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) program with a specialization in Banking and Insurance in Nepal may vary slightly among different universities and colleges. However, I can provide you with a general outline of the BBA in Banking and Insurance curriculum commonly followed in Nepal. Here is an example of the typical curricular structure:

1. Foundation Courses:

  • English
  • Nepali
  • Mathematics
  • Computer Application
  • Basic Economics
  • Business Communication Skills

2. Core Courses:

  • Principles of Management
  • Financial Accounting
  • Cost and Management Accounting
  • Business Statistics
  • Microeconomics
  • Macroeconomics
  • Business Mathematics and Statistics
  • Business Law
  • Organizational Behavior
  • Marketing Management
  • Human Resource Management
  • Financial Management
  • Operations Management
  • Strategic Management

3. Specialization Courses:

  • Introduction to Banking
  • Principles of Insurance
  • Banking Operations and Services
  • Insurance Operations and Services
  • Risk Management in Banking and Insurance
  • Bank Lending and Credit Management
  • Insurance Underwriting and Claims Management
  • Banking Laws and Regulations
  • Insurance Laws and Regulations
  • Investment and Portfolio Management in Banking and Insurance
  • Treasury Management in Banking and Insurance
  • International Banking and Insurance

4. Elective Courses:

Students may have the option to choose elective courses based on their interests and career goals. These courses can be from various business disciplines or other related fields.

5. Internship/Project Work:

BBA programs with a specialization in Banking and Insurance typically include an internship or project work component, where students gain practical experience in a bank, insurance company, or related financial institution. This provides them with opportunities to apply their theoretical knowledge and develop essential skills specific to the banking and insurance industry.

6. Capstone Course:

Towards the end of the program, students may be required to complete a capstone course or a research project, which integrates their learning and demonstrates their understanding of banking and insurance concepts.

It's important to note that the specific courses and their order can vary depending on the institution. Additionally, the total credit hours required to complete a BBA in Banking and Insurance program in Nepal typically range from around 120 to 150 credits, which are spread across several semesters.

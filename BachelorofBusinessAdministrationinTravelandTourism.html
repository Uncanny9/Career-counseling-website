Bachelor of Business Administration in Travel and Tourism

Bachelor of Travel and Tourism Management (BTTM) is a four-year full-time semester system course offered by Tribhuvan University. The program is designed to produce professional human resources for the dynamically growing travel and tourism sector in Nepal. Upon completion of the course, students will be equipped to handle international tourist service operations, including tour planning, trekking programs, and rafting programs. They will also be skilled in domestic and international air ticketing, tourism development planning, sustainable tourism management, and can pursue further advanced courses in travel and tourism management.

The BTTM program aims to prepare students for middle-management positions in the hospitality industry and the travel and tourism sectors. Graduates will possess a comprehensive understanding of the theoretical and practical aspects of the industry. They will be capable of managing and planning tours, treks, and rafting programs, as well as handling domestic and international air ticketing. The program also covers topics such as tourism marketing, tourism development planning, and management. Students will have the opportunity to pursue advanced courses in travel and tourism management and develop entrepreneurial skills applicable to the industry.


ELIGIBILITY

Students who have passed the 10+2 or equivalent examinations from recognized academic institutes with a second division (securing 45% and above) or a CGPA of 1.8 are eligible to enroll in this program. Additionally, students must have taken English as a full paper at the 10+2 or equivalent level.

JOB PROSPECTS

The travel and tourism industry is experiencing rapid growth both in Nepal and abroad. Graduates of the BTTM program are well-prepared to join entry-level positions in banking, finance, and insurance organizations. With their education and training in the conceptual and operational aspects of these sectors, BBA-BI graduates are competent professionals who can excel in such organizations. Additionally, they can work in various functional areas of organizations, whether private or public, domestic or international.


CURRICULAR STRUCTURE

The curricular structure of the Bachelor in Travel and Tourism Management program in Nepal may vary slightly among different universities and colleges. However, here is a general outline of the commonly followed curriculum:

1. Foundation Courses:

  • English
  • Nepali
  • Mathematics
  • Computer Application
  • Basic Economics
  • Business Communication Skills

2. Core Courses:

  • Introduction to Travel and Tourism Industry
  • Tourism Marketing and Promotion
  • Tourism Planning and Development
  • Tourism Economics
  • Principles of Management
  • Accounting for Travel and Tourism
  • Hospitality Management
  • Transportation and Travel Management
  • Tour Operations Management
  • Destination Management
  • Sustainable Tourism Development
  • Event Management in Tourism
  • Cross-Cultural Issues in Tourism
  • Tourism Research Methods

3. Specialization Courses:

  • Adventure Tourism Management
  • Cultural Tourism Management
  • Eco-Tourism and Sustainable Tourism Management
  • Hospitality Management
  • Airline and Airport Management

4. Elective Courses:

Students may have the option to choose elective courses from various business or tourism-related disciplines, allowing them to customize their learning experience.

5. Internship/Project Work:

Bachelor in Travel and Tourism Management programs typically include an internship or project work component, where students gain practical experience in travel agencies, tour operators, hotels, or other tourism-related organizations. This provides them with hands-on industry exposure and the opportunity to apply their knowledge in real-world settings.

6. Capstone Course:

Towards the end of the program, students may be required to complete a capstone course or a research project, which integrates their learning and demonstrates their understanding of travel and tourism management concepts.

It's important to note that the specific courses and their order can vary depending on the institution. Additionally, the total credit hours required to complete a Bachelor in Travel and Tourism Management program in Nepal typically range from around 120 to 150 credits, which are spread across several semesters.

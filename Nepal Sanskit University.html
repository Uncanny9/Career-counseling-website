Nepal Sanskrit University

Nepal Sanskrit University (Nepali: नेपाल संस्कृत विश्वविद्यालय) is one of the universities in Nepal. It was established in December 1986 and has its central office located at Beljhundi in Dang district of Lumbini Province, Nepal. The university is situated 13 km away from the city of Ghorahi.

The university offers Bachelor (Shastri), Bachelor of Education, Masters (Acharya), and Doctoral courses in classical and modern subjects. It also offers a Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS) program and plans to establish the Institute of Sthapatya (IOS) for condensed courses in Ayurvedacharya.

Nepal Sanskrit University has 12 constituent campuses and 13 affiliated Vidhyapeetha (Campuses) throughout the country.

The establishment of the university was inspired by the rich and unbroken tradition of Sanskrit in Nepal. It serves the following purposes:

- Fulfilling the need for an autonomous institution for teaching/learning activities and research of Sanskrit at various levels.
- Systematizing Sanskrit education up to the highest level in the country.
- Preserving and promoting Sanskrit education in Nepalese society.
- Developing Nepal as a center for learning through Sanskrit education.

NSU provides facilities for both degree and non-degree research work based on Sanskrit. It organizes training programs on Vedic and Buddhist teachings, as well as Yoga training.

The chancellor of Nepal Sanskrit University is the Prime Minister, while the Minister for Education serves as the pro-chancellor. The vice-chancellor is responsible for the university's administration. On 12 May 2002, a fire broke out in the university, causing damage worth 27.5 million rupees.

Nepal Sanskrit University offers a range of courses in classical and modern subjects. Here are the main courses available at the university:

1. Bachelor's Degree Programs:
- Bachelor of Arts in Sanskrit (Shastri)
- Bachelor of Education (B.Ed.)

2. Master's Degree Programs:
- Master of Arts in Sanskrit (Acharya)

3. Doctoral Programs:
- Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Sanskrit

4. Ayurvedic Medicine Program:
- Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (BAMS)

In addition to the above programs, Nepal Sanskrit University also offers condensed courses for Ayurvedacharya (Ayurvedic practitioners). The university aims to provide comprehensive education and research opportunities in the field of Sanskrit and related disciplines.

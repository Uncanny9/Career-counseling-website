Bachelor of Health Care Management

Pokhara University launched the program of Bachelor of Health Care Management (BHCM) in 2000 AD to fulfill the growing need for qualified health care professionals with health care management/administration degrees. This 8 Semesters (4 Years) program prepares students to enter management practice in a health care setting.

Different health care companies, hospitals, and organizations related to health care management are in need of qualified and trained candidates with health care management/administration degrees.

The Bachelor of Health Care Management (BHCM) program aims to fulfill its mission through several objectives. Firstly, it strives to develop competent and skilled professionals in the field of health care management and administration. Secondly, the program aims to familiarize students with contemporary principles, concepts, and methods of managing health care systems. Additionally, it seeks to cultivate awareness and passion among students regarding the crucial role health care management plays in advancing the country's development and well-being of its citizens. Lastly, the BHCM program aims to equip students with the necessary foundation to pursue further studies and research at higher levels, such as Master's and Ph.D. degrees, in the field of health care management.

ELIGIBILITY

Pass Grade 12 examination with a minimum CGPA 1.8 and achieve a minimum of D grade in every subject in Grade 11 and 12.

Based on performance on written tests and personal interviews, the final selection will be made.

JOB PROSPECTS

The need for qualified health care professionals with health care management/administration degrees is growing faster than their production in this area. Different health care companies and organizations related to health care management are in need of qualified and trained candidates with health care management/administration degrees. Graduates of health care management programs will have the opportunity to explore exciting careers in:

  • Clinics and Specialty outpatient clinics
  • Health Care Homes
  • Hospices
  • Insurance
  • Mental Health Hospitals

CURRICULAR STRUCTURE

The curricular structure of the Bachelor of Health Care Management (BHCM) program offered by Pokhara University in Nepal typically follows a four-year course of study. The program consists of a combination of core courses, elective courses, internships, and research work. While the specific course titles and order may vary, here is a general outline of the BHCM curriculum:

  1. Foundation Courses:
    • English
    • Nepali
    • Mathematics
    • Introduction to Information Technology
    • Sociology
  2. Core Courses:
    • Principles of Management
    • Principles of Accounting
    • Microeconomics
    • Organizational Behavior
    • Business Communication
    • Health Economics
    • Health Care System and Policy
    • Health Care Quality Management
    • Health Care Ethics
    • Health Promotion and Disease Prevention
    • Health Care Planning and Evaluation
    • Health Information Management Systems
    • Epidemiology and Biostatistics
    • Research Methods in Health Sciences
    • Health Care Leadership and Governance
    • Strategic Management in Health Care
  3. Elective Courses:
    • Students may have the option to choose elective courses based on their interests and career goals. These courses can be from areas such as health finance, health marketing, health information technology, human resource management, or other related fields.
  4. Internship:
    • BHCM programs typically include an internship component where students gain practical experience in health care settings. This provides them with opportunities to apply their theoretical knowledge and develop essential skills.
  5. Research Project:
    • Towards the end of the program, students are usually required to complete a research project related to health care management. This allows them to conduct independent research and contribute to the field.

It's important to note that the specific courses and their order can vary depending on the academic year and the discretion of the university. Additionally, the total credit hours required to complete the BHCM program at Pokhara University typically range from around 120 to 126 credits.
